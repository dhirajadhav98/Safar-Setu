About Trimbakeshwar Temple

Trimbakeshwar Temple is one of the holiest Hindu temples in India and one of the 12 Jyotirlingas where Lord Shiva is worshipped. Located at the source of the Godavari River, this ancient temple dates back to the 18th century in its current form, but the site has been revered for millennia.

The temple features black stone architecture with intricate carvings in the Nagara style. The unique aspect of this Jyotirlinga is that it has three faces embodying Lord Brahma, Lord Vishnu, and Lord Rudra (Shiva), representing the holy trinity of Hindu mythology.

The Godavari River, also known as the Ganga of the South, originates near the temple at Brahmagiri Hills, making this location even more sacred. Pilgrims visit not only to worship the Jyotirlinga but also to pay homage to the holy river at its source.

Religious Significance

As one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, Trimbakeshwar holds immense religious significance for followers of Shiva. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva granted a boon to Gautama Maharishi here, and the River Godavari originated at this place to purify and absolve his sins.

The name "Trimbakeshwar" comes from the three lingas representing the trinity - Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh (Shiva). The temple is also associated with Ardhanareeshwara (half male and half female form of Shiva), symbolizing the unity of masculine and feminine energies.

The temple is also known for its silver masks that adorn the Jyotirlinga and the crown of gold with diamonds that is used on special occasions.

Plan Your Visit

The temple is approximately 28 km from Nashik city and is easily accessible by road. The best time to visit is during the early morning for darshan or during festivals like Mahashivratri when the temple is elaborately decorated.

Visitors should be aware that the temple follows strict dress codes and traditional rules. Men are required to remove their upper garments before entering the inner sanctum, and women should dress modestly. Photography is not allowed inside the main temple.

Accommodation options are available near the temple for pilgrims who wish to stay longer. Many devotees also take a trek to Brahmagiri, the source of the Godavari, which offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills.
