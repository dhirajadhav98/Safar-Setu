About Sai Baba Temple Shirdi

The Sai Baba Temple in Shirdi is one of India's most famous and visited religious sites, dedicated to Shirdi Sai Baba, a spiritual master who lived in the late 19th and early 20th century. Located in the small town of Shirdi, about 83 kilometers from Nashik, this temple attracts millions of devotees annually from all over the world.

The main shrine, known as the Samadhi Mandir, houses the marble statue of Sai Baba sitting on a stone platform. This statue was installed in 1954 and is positioned exactly where Sai Baba's body was buried after he attained mahasamadhi (left his physical body) in 1918. The temple complex has been gradually expanded over the years to accommodate the ever-growing number of devotees.

The architecture of the temple combines elements of Hindu and Islamic designs, reflecting Sai Baba's philosophy of unity among different religions. The temple complex includes several buildings and areas significant to Sai Baba's life, including Dwarkamai, the mosque where he lived for many years.

Spiritual Significance

Sai Baba is revered by both Hindu and Muslim devotees and advocated for unity among all religions. His teachings were simple yet profound, focusing on love, charity, forgiveness, inner peace, and devotion to God. He often summarized his teachings with the phrases "Sabka Malik Ek" (One God governs all) and "Shraddha Saburi" (Faith and Patience).

Devotees believe that Sai Baba continues to help those who pray to him with pure intent. Many miracles are attributed to him, both during his lifetime and after his mahasamadhi. The temple is considered a place where wishes are fulfilled and where spiritual healing takes place.

The Udi (sacred ash) from the perpetual sacred fire (dhuni) that Sai Baba maintained is considered to have healing properties and is distributed to devotees as prasad. The Thursday Palki procession, when a palanquin carrying Sai Baba's portrait is taken around the temple, is an important weekly ritual that attracts thousands.

Plan Your Visit

The Shirdi Sai Baba Temple is open for darshan (viewing of the deity) almost 24 hours a day, with specific rituals and prayers performed at different times. The Kakad Aarti (morning ritual) at 4:30 AM and the Shej Aarti (night ritual) at 10:30 PM are particularly significant and attract many devotees.

Visitors can participate in various activities including the daily aartis, prasad distribution, and bhajan sessions. The temple administration offers accommodation facilities ranging from budget to luxury options for pilgrims planning overnight stays. Online booking is available and recommended, especially during peak seasons and festivals.

The temple is particularly crowded during festivals like Ram Navami, Guru Purnima, and Vijayadashami, as well as on Thursdays (considered Sai Baba's day) and weekends. For a more peaceful experience, visiting on weekdays and during non-festival periods is recommended.

Key Locations in the Temple Complex

Samadhi Mandir: The main shrine where Sai Baba's statue is placed over his final resting place.

Dwarkamai: The mosque where Sai Baba lived for many years and maintained a sacred fire (dhuni).

Gurusthan: The place where Sai Baba was first seen sitting under a neem tree as a young fakir.

Chavadi: A small building where Sai Baba used to sleep on alternate nights.

Lendi Baug: A garden where Sai Baba used to take walks and meditate.

Museum: Houses artifacts associated with Sai Baba, including his personal belongings.
