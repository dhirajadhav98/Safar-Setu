About Saptsrungi Temple

Saptsrungi Temple is a revered Hindu shrine located atop the Saptsrungi hill near Vani in the Nashik district of Maharashtra. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Saptsrungi Devi, also known as Saptashrungi Nivasini, who is considered an incarnation of Goddess Durga. The name "Saptsrungi" derives from 'Sapt' meaning seven and 'Srungi' meaning peaks, as the temple is situated among seven hills.

This ancient temple stands at an impressive height of 1,350 meters (4,430 feet) above sea level, making it one of the highest temples in Maharashtra. The temple structure features traditional Hemadpanthi architecture with intricate stone carvings and a spacious courtyard. The main sanctum houses the revered idol of Goddess Saptsrungi Devi with eighteen arms, each holding different divine weapons.

Pilgrims reach the temple by climbing 108 stone steps, symbolizing the 108 sacred names of the Goddess. The journey uphill is considered a spiritual experience in itself, with devotees chanting prayers during their ascent. A ropeway facility is also available for those who cannot climb the stairs.

Religious Significance

Saptsrungi Temple is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas (holy places of cosmic power) mentioned in ancient Hindu texts. According to mythology, it is believed that the right hand of Goddess Sati fell here when Lord Shiva carried her charred body in grief across the universe.

The temple is particularly important during Navratri (nine nights) festivals when thousands of devotees flock to seek blessings from the Goddess. The temple is also mentioned in the Devi Mahatmya section of the Markandeya Purana, further enhancing its religious significance.

Many devotees visit with specific wishes, and it's a tradition to tie a coconut with red cloth to a tree near the temple when making a wish. Upon fulfillment of their desires, devotees return to offer thanks and perform special pujas.

Plan Your Visit

The temple is located approximately 60 km from Nashik city and is accessible by road. The best time to visit is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is pleasant. During Navratri (usually occurring in September-October or March-April), the temple witnesses huge crowds, so plan accordingly.

Visitors should dress modestly out of respect for the religious site. Comfortable footwear is recommended for climbing the stairs, though you may need to remove shoes before entering the main temple area. The ropeway service operates from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM and requires a separate ticket.

Basic accommodation facilities are available near the temple complex for pilgrims. Food stalls serving simple vegetarian meals and snacks can be found along the path to the temple. The viewpoints near the temple offer breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills, making it worth bringing a camera for photography enthusiasts.
