Saputara Hill Station

Nestled in the Dang district of Gujarat, Saputara is a picturesque hill station located in the Sahyadri mountain range, offering a refreshing escape with its lush green landscapes, pleasant climate, and serene environment.

About Saputara

Saputara, meaning "Abode of Serpents" in the local dialect, is a captivating hill station situated at an altitude of approximately 1,000 meters above sea level. Located in the Dang district of Gujarat, this verdant destination offers a perfect retreat from the heat and hustle of city life.

The hill station is known for its pristine natural beauty, with verdant forests, rolling hills, and stunning viewpoints. It is particularly popular among nature lovers, trekkers, and those seeking a peaceful getaway. The area is home to the Dang tribal community, adding a rich cultural dimension to its natural charm.

Saputara experiences a pleasant climate throughout the year, making it an attractive destination for tourists. During monsoons, the region transforms into a lush green paradise, with waterfalls cascading down the hills and mist covering the landscape.

Natural Attractions

The hill station boasts numerous natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination. The Saputara Lake is a central attraction, offering boating facilities and picturesque views. Sunset Point provides breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and is a favorite spot for photographers and nature enthusiasts.

Gira Falls, located near Saputara, is another stunning natural wonder. During the monsoon season, the waterfall becomes a spectacular sight, with water gushing down from a height of about 30 meters. The surrounding forest area is rich in biodiversity and offers excellent opportunities for nature walks and bird watching.

Plan Your Visit

Saputara is accessible by road from major cities like Surat and Nashik. The nearest railway station is Waghai, about 50 kilometers away. The best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is most pleasant.

Visitors should carry light woolens, comfortable walking shoes, and rain gear during monsoon. There are numerous resorts and hotels in Saputara catering to different budgets. The local cuisine, influenced by tribal culture, is another highlight of the visit.
